Reclaiming our story: one student at a time.

Diverse stories. Told by us for us.

iFeminist empowers women by exposing them to women in history as well as enabling them to conduct their own research. iFeminist encourages research on diverse women from around the world and throughout time to change the narrative regarding women's contributions to society. We are students aimed at changing the face of history by conducting our own research projects.

Our Team

Saba Mehrzad

Founder, Editor in Chief, and Head Software Developer

Saba is a junior at Syosset High School in Syosset, NY.

She first started studying women in history when she was accepted as an intern at the New Historia under Women's Studies Professor Dr. Walker. She is excited to learn more about the women that paved the way for her! In her free time, she likes to celebrate her Iranian heritage with her Iranian teen group, go to the science laboratory, code, and watch old movies!

Once she started studying Sedigheh Dolatabadi, she started to truly love, cherish, and appreciate her background because of Dolatabadi's amazing accomplishments. She wants everyone to feel empowered by their identity and feels as though studying Iranian women in history did that for her.

Saba's work under Dr. Walker focused on Iranian journalist and philanthropist, Sedigheh Dolatabadi. It is published here! Check it out on the Discoveries page.

Giovanna Napoleone

Student Researcher

Giovanna is a junior at Syosset High School in Syosset, NY.

Giovanna has always been interested in learning more about her Latin American heritage and the history of women in medicine. Giovanna was excited for an opportunity to study a pioneering physician from Ecuador, the country her mother is from.

In her free time, Giovanna is a part of her high school’s robotics team, PreMed Society, and Oratory speech team where she learns how to collaborate with others. She also enjoys playing the violin and volunteering at her local church to teach first graders on the weekends.

For iFeminist, Giovanna researched an Ecuadorian physician and poet named Matilde Hidalgo de Procel who became the first woman in Ecuador to vote. More information about Matilde Hidalgo de Procel is published on this site’s Discoveries page, so feel free to check it out!
